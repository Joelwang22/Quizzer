12  A. The action is morally permissible.  B. The action is morally required.  C. The action is morally wrong.  D. The action is morally neutral.  ✓ E. We need more information to determine the moral status of this action.  Answer: E. No partial credits.  According to classic utilitarianism, a right action is an action that maximizes total net pleasure among the available actions in the given context. Since we don’t know what the other available actions are, we cannot determine the moral status of this action. If the other available actions all produce less total net pleasure than 0 (i.e., if they produce more pain than pleasure), this action would be right. If there is an available action that produces more total net pleasure than 0, this action would be wrong.

This course provides an introduction to philosophy.  Philosophy explores, using reason and argument, important questions that other disciplines are unwilling or unable to answer, such as…  Is abortion morally wrong?  Science can tell us a lot of facts about the fetus (e.g., when the fetus’s heartbeat starts),  but those facts alone do not answer whether abortion is morally wrong or not.  This because, for instance, we would still need to know whether having a heartbeat has a moral significance or, to put it another way, whether it is always wrong to kill something that has a heartbeat (and if not, exactly when it is wrong to do so).

--- Page 24 ---
This course provides an introduction to philosophy.  Philosophy explores, using reason and argument, important questions that other disciplines are unwilling or unable to answer, such as…  Is abortion morally wrong?  Science can tell us a lot of facts about the fetus (e.g., when the fetus’s heartbeat starts),  but those facts alone do not answer whether abortion is morally wrong or not.  This because, for instance, we would still need to know whether having a heartbeat has a moral significance or, to put it another way, whether it is always wrong to kill something that has a heartbeat (and if not, exactly when it is wrong to do so).  We cannot answer these questions by doing more scientific research.  Answering such questions is what philosophy aims to do.
